Explore the Untamed Beauty of the Sundarban Tour

The Sundarban, a name synonymous with mystery and adventure, is a sprawling delta formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. Spanning across India and Bangladesh, the Sundarban is home to the world’s largest mangrove forest and the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger. Our Sundarban tour packages offer a unique opportunity to explore this UNESCO World Heritage Site, where nature reigns supreme.

Rich Biodiversity

The Sundarban is a treasure trove of biodiversity, hosting over 400 species of wildlife, including endangered species like the Royal Bengal Tiger, Estuarine Crocodile, and Gangetic Dolphin.

Unique Ecosystem

The mangrove forests of Sundarban are unlike any other, with a complex ecosystem that supports a diverse range of flora and fauna. The intricate network of rivers, creeks, and estuaries creates a landscape that is both beautiful and mysterious.

Cultural Richness

Beyond its natural beauty, Sundarban is also home to indigenous communities that have lived in harmony with the forest for centuries. Their customs, traditions, and folklore add a rich cultural dimension to your Sundarban tour.

Best Time to Visit Sundarban

More than 5 year experience in this profession

The best time to visit Sundarban is between November and March. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the chances of spotting wildlife, including the Royal Bengal Tiger, are higher. The monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rainfall, making the area less accessible, but it also transforms the landscape into a lush, green paradise.

Explore Sundarban's Unique Attractions

Sundarban National Park

The heart of the Sundarban, this national park is home to a vast array of wildlife, including the Royal Bengal Tiger. Explore the park's waterways on a boat safari and immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the mangroves.

Sajnekhali Watchtower

One of the best spots for wildlife viewing, the Sajnekhali Watchtower offers a chance to see tigers, deer, and a variety of birds. The tower is also home to a small museum and a crocodile park.

Dobanki Watchtower

This watchtower features a canopy walk that takes you above the forest, offering a bird's-eye view of the surroundings. It's a great spot for photography and bird watching.
